# Respiratory Triage Guidelines

## **Class 1: HOSPITAL EMERGENCY**

* **Severe Respiratory Distress**
  * Marked retractions, nasal flaring, cyanosis.
  * Moderate–severe distress not improving after bronchodilator.
  * Respiratory difficulty in infants <4 months.
* **Airway Obstruction**
  * Moderate–severe stridor at rest or suspected obstruction.
  * Choking with color change (blue/gray).
* **High-Risk Patterns**
  * Apnea or respiratory pauses.
  * Respiratory difficulty in patients with underlying disease and potential complications.

## **Class 2: PRIMARY CARE / URGENT CARE (SAME-DAY VISIT)**

* **Infections & Symptoms**
  * Common cold or viral pharyngitis with high fever.
  * Suspected pertussis.
  * Mild–moderate respiratory difficulty improving with treatment.
* **Specific Symptoms**
  * Cough in infants <12 months without significant distress.
  * Hemoptysis (coughing up blood).

## **Class 3: PRIMARY CARE / NON-URGENT (7–10 DAY VISIT)**

* **Minor Symptoms**
  * Mild upper respiratory symptoms without fever.
  * Seasonal allergies.
