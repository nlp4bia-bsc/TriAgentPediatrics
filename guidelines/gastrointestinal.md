# Gastrointestinal Triage Guidelines

## **Class 1: HOSPITAL EMERGENCY**

* **Acute Abdomen & Emergencies**
  * Severe abdominal pain or surgical emergency symptoms.
  * Acute abdomen (guarding, rigidity, rebound tenderness).
  * Suspected appendicitis, intussusception, obstruction, or incarcerated hernia.
  * Pyloric stenosis signs (infant <1 month with projectile vomiting).
* **Dehydration**
  * Moderate–severe dehydration not responding to oral rehydration.

## **Class 2: PRIMARY CARE / URGENT CARE (SAME-DAY VISIT)**

* **Symptomatic Management**
  * Moderate diarrhea (≥4 episodes/day) without severe dehydration.
  * Mild diarrhea in infants <24 months lasting <5 days without alarm signs.
  * Vomiting without dehydration.
  * Mild–moderate abdominal pain.
  * Rectal bleeding with good general condition.

## **Class 3: PRIMARY CARE / NON-URGENT (7–10 DAY VISIT)**

* **Minor GI Issues**
  * Viral infections without alarm signs.
  * Mild or chronic abdominal pain.
  * Mild diarrhea in infants <24 months lasting ≥5 days; or mild diarrhea in children >24 months.
  * Constipation, gas, fecal leakage.
  * Uncomplicated hernias.
  * Perianal pain or itching.
