# Trauma & Musculoskeletal Triage Guidelines

## **Class 1: HOSPITAL EMERGENCY**

* **Severe Trauma**
  * Polytrauma.
  * Head trauma with risk signs (loss of consciousness, repeated vomiting, confusion).
  * Trauma in patients with coagulation disorders.
  * Trauma suggestive of abuse (requiring expert teams).
* **Fractures & Wounds**
  * Suspected fracture, open fractures, or severe deformities.
  * Deep wounds.
  * Persistent heavy bleeding (requiring >10 min pressure).
* **Burns**
  * Severe burns (extensive 2nd/3rd degree or involving face/hands/feet/genitals).

## **Class 2: PRIMARY CARE / URGENT CARE (SAME-DAY VISIT)**

* **Minor Injuries**
  * Superficial cuts without heavy bleeding.
  * Mild contusions.
  * Minor burns.
  * Animal bites or small insect stings.
* **Falls**
  * Falls in infants <1 year without head trauma or fracture signs.

## **Class 3: PRIMARY CARE / NON-URGENT (7–10 DAY VISIT)**

* **Minor Musculoskeletal**
  * Mild pain anywhere.
  * Muscle cramps.
  * Falls in children >1 year without head trauma or fracture signs.
  * Skull deformities without trauma history.
