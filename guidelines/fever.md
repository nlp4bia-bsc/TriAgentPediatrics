# Fever Triage Guidelines

## **Class 1: HOSPITAL EMERGENCY**

* **High Fever with Signs of Sepsis or in a Vulnerable Patient**
  * Fever in infants under 3 months of age.
  * Signs of sepsis: lethargy, poor perfusion, extreme irritability, non-blanching skin rash (petechiae/purpura).
  * Fever of any degree in a patient with a known vulnerability: primary or secondary immunosuppression, neonate.
  * Persistent fever without a clear source for more than 7 consecutive days, with tests so far being negative.

## **Class 2: PRIMARY CARE / URGENT CARE (SAME-DAY VISIT)**

* **Fever Without Signs of Sepsis**
  * Fever in an infant (3-24 months) for more than 24 hours without alarm signs.
  * Fever in a child over 24 months old, lasting between 3 and 7 days, without alarm signs.
  * Fever associated with other alarm symptoms not related to sepsis (e.g., respiratory, skin symptoms).

## **Class 3: PRIMARY CARE / NON-URGENT (7–10 DAY VISIT)**

* **Uncomplicated Fever**
  * Fever for less than 3 days in a child over 24 months old without any alarm signs.
  * Fever for less than 24 hours in an infant (3-24 months) without any alarm signs.
