# Neurological Triage Guidelines

## **Class 1: HOSPITAL EMERGENCY**

* **Alterations in Consciousness or Neurological Function**
  * Active, prolonged (>5 minutes), or multiple seizures in a short period.
  * Decreased level of consciousness or diminished response (Glasgow Coma Scale <13).
  * Sudden changes in mental state, excessive irritability/somnolence.
  * Sudden difficulty speaking, sudden loss of mobility/sensation in a part of the body, ataxia, gait disturbance.
  * Signs of meningitis (neck stiffness, bulging fontanelle, petechiae with fever).
  * Severe headache (VAS >7) that does not respond to analgesia or is accompanied by other neurological focal signs.

## **Class 2: PRIMARY CARE / URGENT CARE (SAME-DAY VISIT)**

* **Moderate Neurological Disorders**
  * Mild to moderate headache.
  * A single seizure lasting less than 5 minutes that stopped spontaneously with complete recovery, in the context of a typical febrile seizure or a patient with a known history of seizures.
  * Dizziness or vertigo.
  * Isolated paresthesia (tingling/numbness).

## **Class 3: PRIMARY CARE / NON-URGENT (7–10 DAY VISIT)**

* **Minor Neurological Disorders**
  * Loss of smell or taste.
  * Tics without other associated symptoms.
  * Sleep disorders without alarm signs.
  * Mild headache without other neurological symptoms.
